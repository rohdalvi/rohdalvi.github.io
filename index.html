Experience

In the summer of 2019, I interned at BorgWarner Inc, a Tier 1 automotive supplier in Auburn Hills, MI. Here, I performed a number of tasks and strengthened a variety of skills. As a member of the Noise, Vibration, and Harshness group, I performed a long-term project where I investigated abnormal torque fluctuations using HEAD Acoustics Artemis Suite. As a member of the Validation group, I wrote a MATLAB script that could rapidly process test data with the capability to save 30 minutes per data set.
Michigan Electric Racing is a student-led design team that competes in SAE Formula Electric. Currently, I am working on creating the car's dashboard and integrating the dashboard electronics. In the past, I lead the design of the steering system. Through this role, I have accomplished a number of tasks such as using MATLAB to analyze tire data for Ackerman angle calculations, using Siemens NX to CAD our steering system, and using ANSYS to ensure its strength. Additionally, I have learned a great deal about effective leadership and communication. The club has allowed me to gain extensive hands-on experience by applying theory learned in the classroom to real-world situations and problems. Furthermore, I have loved motorsports since I was a small child and MER has given me the opportunity to be a part of that world.
Northville High School Aerospace Club was founded in 2014 to provide opportunities for students to explore the field of aerospace with hands-on projects. I was one of the cofounders of this club, and also served as president. My experiences ranged from designing, assembling, and helping launch a weather balloon to acquiring a historic project aircraft and a hangar to store it in. The Village Workshop is a DIY space in my hometown of Northville, Michigan where people can come in and work on their personal projects. I completed an internship here which gave me a solid foundation in real-world design and manufacturing. For the workshop, I designed, machined, and painted a sign to advertise lumber for sale using Autodesk Inventor and a CNC machine. I also machined and assembled a cart for patrons to use to move plywood around the workshop. This experience was a great introduction to a workshop environment and I was able to learn how to operate basic workshop machinery and power tools efficiently and safely.
